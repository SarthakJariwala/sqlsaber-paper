Here is the SQL query to get this information. Since SQLite doesn't natively support `YEAR()` or `SUBTRACT()` functions, we use `STRFTIME` and standard math subtraction to calculate their age, which behaves exactly as requested.

```sql
SELECT DISTINCT T1.ID, 
       CASE 
         WHEN T1.Admission = '+' THEN 'Admitted'
         WHEN T1.Admission = '-' THEN 'Not Admitted'
         ELSE 'Unknown/Other'
       END AS Admission_Status
FROM Patient AS T1
JOIN Laboratory AS T2 
  ON T1.ID = T2.ID
WHERE T1.SEX = 'F' 
  AND CAST(STRFTIME('%Y', 'now') AS INTEGER) - CAST(STRFTIME('%Y', T1.Birthday) AS INTEGER) >= 50
  AND (T2.RBC <= 3.5 OR T2.RBC >= 6.0);
```

### Explanation of the Query:
1. **`JOIN Laboratory... ON`**: Merges patient profile data with their lab records based on their ID.
2. **`WHERE T1.SEX = 'F'`**: Filters the list strictly to female patients.
3. **`CAST(STRFTIME('%Y', 'now') AS INTEGER) - ... >= 50`**: Extracts the year of birth and the current year to calculate their age, checking if they are 50 or older.
4. **`AND (T2.RBC <= 3.5 OR T2.RBC >= 6.0)`**: Checks their lab history to confirm they had at least one abnormal red blood cell count recording.
5. **`CASE WHEN ...`**: Translates the dataset's `+` and `-` indicators into a readable "Admitted" and "Not Admitted" format. 
6. **`DISTINCT`**: Ensures that even if a patient had multiple tests with abnormal RBCs over time, they are only listed once in the final result.
