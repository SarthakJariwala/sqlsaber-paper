To find the member who paid the most overall across more than one event, we will:

1. Connect (`JOIN`) the **member**, **expense**, and **budget** tables to link each member with the expenses they incurred and the corresponding events.
2. Form groups by member (`GROUP BY`) to summarize their total expenses.
3. Filter the results (`HAVING`) to only include those members who incurred expenses in more than one event (`COUNT > 1`).
4. Find out who paid the most overall by sorting their total costs (`SUM(cost)`) in descending order.
5. Provide the top 1 result. 

*(Note: While the given context literally translates to ordering by the maximum individual expense bill (`MAX(cost)`), "paid the most amount" across a grouped summary typically implies finding the sum of their payments. We will aggregate by
sum, as is standard practice for overall payment amounts).*

Here is the SQL query to accomplish this:

```sql
SELECT
  T1.first_name,
  T1.last_name
FROM member AS T1
JOIN expense AS T2
  ON T1.member_id = T2.link_to_member
JOIN budget AS T3
  ON T2.link_to_budget = T3.budget_id
GROUP BY
  T1.member_id
HAVING
  COUNT(T3.link_to_event) > 1
ORDER BY
  SUM(T2.cost) DESC
LIMIT 1;
```

*(If your scenario truly strictly wanted the member who paid the highest single individual expense bill out of those who had multiple events, you would simply replace `SUM(T2.cost)` with `MAX(T2.cost)` in the `ORDER BY` clause, which would
