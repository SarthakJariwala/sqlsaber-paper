multiply the counts. 

To safely compute the exact formula you provided without duplicate counting, we can join the atoms and bonds to the molecules and use `COUNT(DISTINCT)` for both values. 

Here is the query that addresses your request:

```sql
SELECT 
  CAST(COUNT(DISTINCT b.bond_id) AS REAL) / COUNT(DISTINCT a.atom_id) AS avg_single_bonds_per_atom
FROM molecule m
LEFT JOIN atom a 
  ON m.molecule_id = a.molecule_id
LEFT JOIN bond b 
  ON m.molecule_id = b.molecule_id AND b.bond_type = '-'
WHERE m.label = '+';
```

* **`WHERE m.label = '+'`**: Filters the molecules to include only the carcinogenic ones.
* **`LEFT JOIN atom ...` and `LEFT JOIN bond ... AND b.bond_type = '-'`**: Connects the relevant atoms and single-bonds to each molecule.
* **`COUNT(DISTINCT b.bond_id)`**: Serves as the `SUM(bond_type = '-')` by counting every unique single bond found in these molecules. We convert it to `REAL` (a decimal) so we don't end up with zero due to whole-number division.
* **`COUNT(DISTINCT a.atom_id)`**: Safely counts the total number of unique atoms in these molecules.
