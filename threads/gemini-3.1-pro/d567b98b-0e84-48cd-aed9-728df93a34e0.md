Here is the SQL query that answers your question:

```sql
SELECT 
  COUNT(DISTINCT T1.ID)
FROM Patient AS T1
JOIN Laboratory AS T2
  ON T1.ID = T2.ID
WHERE T1.SEX = 'M' 
  AND CAST(SUBSTR(T1.Birthday, 1, 4) AS INTEGER) BETWEEN 1936 AND 1956 
  AND T2.CPK >= 250;
```

**Explanation of the query:**
* **`JOIN Laboratory AS T2 ON T1.ID = T2.ID`**: Connects the patient's demographic information with their corresponding lab test records.
* **`WHERE T1.SEX = 'M'`**: Filters the records to only include male patients.
* **`AND CAST(SUBSTR(T1.Birthday, 1, 4) AS INTEGER) BETWEEN 1936 AND 1956`**: Extracts the 4-digit year from the patient's birth date to ensure they were born between 1936 and 1956. *(Note: Since standard SQLite doesn't have a `YEAR()` 
function, we extract the first 4 characters of the date string).*
* **`AND T2.CPK >= 250`**: Filters for the creatinine phosphokinase levels that are out of the normal range (250 or higher).
* **`COUNT(DISTINCT T1.ID)`**: Counts only unique patients so that individuals with multiple high CPK tests aren't counted more than once.
