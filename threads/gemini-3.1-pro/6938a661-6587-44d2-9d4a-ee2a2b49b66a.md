To find the percentage of male patients who first presented to the hospital in 1981 and were diagnosed with BEHCET, we can query the `Patient` table. 

We will filter the records to match male patients (`SEX = 'M'`) and extract the year 1981 from their first presentation date. Then, we can calculate the percentage by dividing the number of patients diagnosed with BEHCET by the total number
of male patients from that year.

Here is the SQL query that calculates this:

```sql
SELECT 
    CAST(SUM(CASE WHEN Diagnosis = 'BEHCET' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS REAL) * 100 / COUNT(ID) AS percentage 
FROM Patient 
WHERE SEX = 'M' 
  AND strftime('%Y', "First Date") = '1981';
```

### Explanation of the Result:
- **Filtering**: `WHERE SEX = 'M' AND strftime('%Y', "First Date") = '1981'` restricts the data to only male patients whose first recorded date falls within the year 1981.
- **Calculating the Percentage**: The `CASE` statement flags the records where the diagnosis is exactly `'BEHCET'`. We then sum these flagged cases, multiply by 100, and divide by the total number of matching males (`COUNT(ID)`) to compute 
the final percentage. 

**Result:** The calculation results in **50%**. Out of the 4 male patients who first presented in 1981, exactly 2 of them were diagnosed with BEHCET.
