To find the correct percentage, we need to gather the race results from the years 2000 to 2005, check the dates of birth of the drivers, and count how many completed more than 50 laps in those races. 

Here is the SQL query that calculates this based on the formula provided:

```sql
SELECT 
  CAST(SUM(CASE WHEN CAST(strftime('%Y', d.dob) AS INTEGER) < 1985 AND r.laps > 50 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS REAL) * 100.0 / COUNT(r.driverId) AS percentage
FROM results r
JOIN races ra ON r.raceId = ra.raceId
JOIN drivers d ON r.driverId = d.driverId
WHERE ra.year BETWEEN 2000 AND 2005;
```

*   **`WHERE ra.year BETWEEN 2000 AND 2005`**: This filters our total data pool so we are only looking at races that occurred within the target 6-year timeframe (the denominator).
*   **`SUM(CASE WHEN ...)`**: This works as our numerator. It goes through every race entry from 2000–2005 and adds `1` only if the driver's birth year is before 1985 **and** they completed more than 50 laps. 
*   **`* 100.0 / COUNT(r.driverId)`**: This divides our matched records by the total number of driver race entries from 2000–2005, and formats the result as a percentage.
