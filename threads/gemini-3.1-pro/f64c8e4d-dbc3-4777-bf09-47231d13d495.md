Based on the database structure, here is the SQL query to find the average finish time in seconds for the champions of each year prior to 1975:

```sql
SELECT 
    r.year, 
    AVG(res.milliseconds) / 1000.0 AS avg_time_sec 
FROM races r
JOIN results res 
    ON r.raceId = res.raceId
WHERE res.position = 1 
  AND res.time IS NOT NULL 
  AND r.year < 1975
GROUP BY r.year;
```

### **Explanation of the Query:**
1. **`JOIN results res ON r.raceId = res.raceId`**: This connects the individual race results with the general race calendar so we can see the `year` of each race.
2. **`WHERE res.position = 1 AND res.time IS NOT NULL AND r.year < 1975`**: This filters our data to specifically look at only the race winners (champions) who actually finished the race (time is not null) in the years prior to 1975.
3. **`AVG(res.milliseconds) / 1000.0`**: Since the exact champion's finish time (represented as 'HH:MM:SS.mmm') is pre-parsed into the `milliseconds` column, taking the average and dividing it by 1,000 reliably gives us the average time in 
seconds.
4. **`GROUP BY r.year`**: Groups the calculations to output one average per year.
